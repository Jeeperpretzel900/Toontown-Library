Ariana B., 14
My Doodle, Banjo
My Doodle is a rescue, a mutt if you must say.
He is a cross between a sketch and a scribble, I got him just yesterday.

With a full bean bank in hand, I sauntered into the pet store.
There were striped ones and spotted ones, but none that I truly adored.

The day was late in Donald's Dreamland, and I had to go turn in my task.
I wished to see but one more, Clerk R.V. Wrinkle opened the clasp.

His owner abandoned him, and he wasn't very well trained.
With those big brown eyes and longing disposition, an immediate love for him was gained.

It turned out that he rarely forgets and is not ever confused.
He is always affectionate and "sometimes" hungry; although "always" is the word I would use.

While walking through Melodyland the next day, we both saw a dog playing an instrument of some kind.
He turned out to be the greatest banjo player Tenor Terrace could ever find.

My Doodle immediately took a liking and began to dance to the music.
That's why I named him banjo, a perfect name, we both knew it.

He is filled with joy when I am happy, and Toons me up when I am sad.
Although, when he nibbles at my Opera tree, I get pretty mad.

In the end I would never hurt him, and he would never hurt me.
That is why he is the greatest Doodle Toontown has ever seen!

The Legend of Diamond Lil
(With a tip of my J.B. Stetson hat to Robert Fletcher and Cole Porter)
This here's the Legend of Diamond Lil;
I’m here ta tells ya, cuz...I'm Lil…Diamond Lil.
Legend "can't look at hobbles and she can't stand fences," true;
I tells ya, I seen it, that Legend, she just walks on through!

When home on the range, Legend likes ta dig;
When she's a called fer ta save me, Legend does a mighty fine jig!
Legend, she likes ta sleep under starry skies at night;
But she's bin ever faithful ta me when I gits inta a squirt gun slingin' fight.

She's a Legend fer sure, the Legend of free will;
She's a Legend, the Legend of…Diamond Lil.
